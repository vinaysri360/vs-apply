Getting Started
One-Time Setup (10 minutes)
1
Sign in with Google
Click the sign-in button when you first open VS Apply. This authorizes Gmail (for sending emails) and Google Sheets (for tracking applications). Your data stays in your browser — we never store it on any server.
2
Download the resume template
Click "⬇ Download Resume Template" in the welcome screen, or find it inside the profile editor. Open it in Microsoft Word and customize it with your real information — name, contact details, work history sections with company names, roles, dates, and "Responsibilities-" lines. Each client section needs at least one placeholder bullet.
3
Create your profile
Click the profile button in the top-right corner → fill in your name, title, email, phone, LinkedIn, years of experience, skills, and work history with domain descriptions for each client. Upload your customized .docx template. Click Save.
4
Set up Claude.ai
Go to claude.ai → create a new Project → open Project Instructions. In VS Apply, click the 📋 Prompt button (top-right) — this copies your auto-generated master prompt. Paste it into Claude's Project Instructions and save. You only do this once (re-copy if you update your profile).
5
Configure Settings (optional)
Go to ⚙ Settings to add your Google Sheet ID (for application tracking), select your timezone, and set your preferred save filename. If you don't add a Sheet ID, the app works fine — you just won't have cloud backup of your records.
Daily Workflow (60 seconds per application)
1
Find a job posting
On LinkedIn, Dice, Indeed, or any job board. Copy the full job description text.
2
Generate with Claude
Open your Claude.ai project → start a new chat → paste the job description. Claude generates a tailored email, resume bullets, and tracking info using your master prompt.
3
Process in VS Apply
Copy Claude's entire response → click 📋 PASTE in VS Apply → click ⚡ PROCESS. The app parses the output, injects bullets into your .docx template, populates the email fields, and logs the application.
4
Review and send
Check the resume preview in the middle panel. Review the email on the right. Click 💾 Save to save the .docx, then Send Email to send via Gmail with the resume attached. Done.
Features
📄 DOCX Injection
Injects AI-generated bullets directly into your Word template. Preserves formatting, fonts, and layout. Three modes: Replace, Keep+Fixed, Keep+Random.
✉️ Gmail Integration
Send emails with your resume attached directly from the app via Gmail API. No need to open Gmail separately.
📊 Google Sheets Sync
Track every application in a Google Sheet. Auto-creates tabs for Applications, Profiles, Notes, and daily counters.
👤 Multi-Profile
Create profiles for different roles — BA, Data Analyst, Scrum Master. Each has its own prompt, template, skills, and work history.
💾 Smart Save
Uses File System Access API to overwrite the same file every time. No more "Resume (1)(2)(3).docx" cluttering your downloads.
📝 Notes
Built-in note-taking with auto-save and Google Sheets sync. Track interview prep, company research, follow-ups.
🔒 Test Mode
Lock the recipient email to your test address. Process real JDs without accidentally emailing recruiters.
🌍 Timezone Support
14 worldwide timezones. Daily counters reset at midnight in your selected zone.
Resume Template Requirements

Your .docx resume template must follow this structure for bullet injection to work:

📝 Required Structure (per client)
Company Name — plain paragraph, not a bullet
Role Title     Date Range — plain paragraph
Responsibilities- — plain paragraph (this exact word)
• Placeholder bullet — at least one, using Word's built-in bullet list

Repeat for each client. Add an empty line between client sections.
⚠ Common mistake: When adding new client sections in Word, the bullet formatting from the previous section can carry over. Make sure company name, role, and "Responsibilities-" lines are plain paragraphs, not bulleted. Only the placeholder bullets should have bullet formatting.
Data & Privacy
🔐 Your data never leaves your browser
All profiles, application records, notes, and settings are stored in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. Google OAuth is used only to authorize Gmail sending and Sheets sync — and only when you explicitly click Send or Sync.

Profiles & Applications — sync to YOUR Google Sheet via ↑↓ buttons
Notes — stored locally + optional Sheet sync
Settings — localStorage only

If you clear your browser data, your local data is gone. Use Google Sheets sync as your backup.
FAQ
Do I need a Claude.ai account?
Yes. VS Apply uses Claude.ai (free tier works) to generate tailored content from job descriptions. You paste the JD in Claude, copy the output back to VS Apply.
Does this cost anything?
VS Apply itself is completely free. Claude.ai has a free tier with limited messages. For heavy daily use (20+ applications/day), Claude Pro ($20/month) is recommended.
Can I use ChatGPT instead of Claude?
Technically yes — the output format (===EMAIL_START===, ===RESUME_START===, etc.) works with any AI. But the prompt is optimized for Claude and may need adjustments for other models.
Is my Google account safe?
Yes. VS Apply uses Google's official OAuth 2.0 sign-in. Your password is never seen by the app. You authorize specific permissions (Gmail send + Sheets access) through Google's consent screen, and you can revoke access anytime from your Google Account settings.
Can multiple people use this?
Yes. Each person signs in with their own Google account. Data is stored in each person's own browser and synced to their own Google Sheet. There's no shared data between users.
What browsers are supported?
Chrome and Edge (recommended). The File System Access API for smart save only works in Chromium browsers. Safari and Firefox work for everything except smart save — you'll get normal downloads instead.
